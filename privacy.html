Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how INVENEW Inc., a Canada corporation, collects, uses, discloses, retains, transfers, and protects personal information for visitors, readers, subscribers, sponsors, and business contacts in Canada, the United States, Europe, the United Kingdom, and other regions.

Last updated: May 25, 2026

1. Scope and Controller

This policy applies to personal information handled by INVENEW Inc. through this website, INVENEW Intelligence, newsletters, blog content, Labs activity, sponsorship inquiries, forms, and related business communications. INVENEW Inc. is responsible for the personal information it handles. For European and United Kingdom visitors, INVENEW Inc. is the controller for the processing described in this policy unless another party is clearly identified.

2. Privacy Principles

INVENEW aims to follow Canadian private-sector privacy principles, including accountability, identifying purposes, meaningful consent, limiting collection, limiting use and disclosure, retention limits, accuracy, safeguards, openness, access, correction, and complaint handling. Where applicable, we also consider regional privacy rights for European, United Kingdom, and United States audiences.

3. Personal Information We Collect

We may collect information you provide directly, such as name, work email, company, role, sponsorship interest, newsletter subscription details, form submissions, messages, and preferences. We may also receive limited technical information from website, newsletter, hosting, analytics, advertising, security, or embedded tools, such as device type, browser, pages viewed, referral source, approximate location, timestamps, and interaction data.

4. How We Use Personal Information

We use personal information to provide and improve the website, send newsletters, manage subscriptions, respond to sponsorship inquiries, evaluate sponsor fit, manage business relationships, measure content performance, protect systems, prevent abuse, comply with legal obligations, and communicate about INVENEW Intelligence, Labs, blog content, sponsorship opportunities, or related offerings where permitted.

5. Legal Bases and Consent

Depending on your location and the context, we process personal information with your consent, to perform a requested service, for legitimate business interests, to comply with legal obligations, or as otherwise permitted by law. You may withdraw consent where legally permitted, subject to reasonable notice and any legal or contractual restrictions. Newsletter emails include an unsubscribe option.

6. Newsletter and Sponsorship Inquiries

Newsletter subscriptions may be handled by Beehiiv or another newsletter provider so INVENEW can send briefings and related communications. Sponsorship inquiries may be handled through Netlify Forms or another form provider so INVENEW can review fit, respond to requests, discuss options, and manage related business communications. We do not sell sponsorship inquiry information.

7. Service Providers and Disclosures

We may disclose personal information to service providers that help operate the website, host files, process forms, manage newsletters, deliver email, secure systems, measure performance, support analytics, or provide business tools. We may also disclose information if required by law, to protect rights or security, during a business transaction, or with your direction or consent.

8. Cross-Border Processing

INVENEW is based in Canada, but service providers may store or process information in Canada, the United States, Europe, or other jurisdictions. When information is processed outside your province, state, or country, it may be subject to the laws of that jurisdiction. Where required, we use appropriate safeguards for international transfers.

9. Cookies, Analytics, and Similar Technologies

The website may use cookies, embedded forms, analytics tools, or similar technologies to support functionality, understand content performance, improve user experience, and protect the site. You can adjust browser settings to block or delete cookies. Some features may not work as intended if cookies or embedded tools are disabled.

10. Retention

We retain personal information only as long as reasonably necessary for the purposes described in this policy, to meet legal or business requirements, to resolve disputes, or to maintain appropriate records. Information that is no longer required is deleted, anonymized, or securely disposed of.

11. Safeguards

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards appropriate to the sensitivity of the information. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, but we work to protect personal information against loss, theft, unauthorized access, disclosure, copying, use, or modification.

12. Your Privacy Rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, restrict, object to, or receive a copy of your personal information. You may also have the right to withdraw consent, opt out of certain communications, or complain to a privacy regulator. We may need to verify your identity before responding and may refuse or limit requests where permitted by law.

13. Canadian Rights

Canadian individuals may request access to personal information INVENEW holds about them, ask how it has been used or disclosed, request correction of inaccurate information, and raise privacy questions or complaints. INVENEW will review requests and respond as appropriate under applicable Canadian privacy law.

14. European and United Kingdom Rights

If you are in the European Economic Area, Switzerland, or the United Kingdom, you may have rights under applicable data protection law, including access, rectification, erasure, restriction, objection, portability, withdrawal of consent, and the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. Where processing is based on legitimate interests, you may object as permitted by law.

15. United States Privacy Notice

United States residents may have privacy rights under applicable state laws, including rights to know, access, correct, delete, and opt out of certain sales, sharing, targeted advertising, or profiling where those laws apply. INVENEW does not sell personal information for money. If future advertising or analytics practices trigger opt-out rights, INVENEW will provide an appropriate mechanism.

16. Children

INVENEW is intended for business and professional audiences. The website is not directed to children, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

17. Contact and Complaints

Questions, access requests, correction requests, deletion requests, opt-out requests, or privacy complaints should be directed to INVENEW Inc.’s privacy contact through the sponsorship/contact form on this website or another official contact method published by INVENEW. INVENEW will review privacy requests and respond as appropriate.

18. Changes

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The updated version will be posted on this page with a revised “Last updated” date.
